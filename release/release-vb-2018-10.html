Release VB-2018-10

Submitted by rwieck on Tue, 2018-10-16 08:35
We are pleased to announce the October 2018 release of VectorBase (VB-2018-10):
  • In this release there were updates to the gene sets for 24 species based on community annotation ( Anopheles albimanus, An. arabiensis, An. christyi, An. coluzzii, An. culicifacies, An. darlingi, An. dirus, An. epiroticus, An. farauti, An. funestus, An. gambiae, An. melas, An. merus, An. minimus, An. quadriannulatus, An. sinensis (Sinensis strain), An. stephensi (SDA-500 strain), Glossina austeni, G. brevipalpis, G. fuscipes, G. pallidipes, G. palpalis, Lutzomyia longipalpis and Phlebotomus papatasi ).
  • New variation data for Anopheles gambiae populations from around Lake Victoria, Uganda as described in the publication "Reduced-representation sequencing identifies small effective population sizes of Anopheles gambiae in the north-western Lake Victoria basin, Uganda." by Wiltshire et al. (2018)
  • 3 RNA-Seq new track hubs for Aedes aegypti related to Zika and Dengue viruses:
    • Zika & Dengue virus infections (Anglero-rodriguez et al., Front Microbiol. 2017)
    • DENV1 and DENV3 infections (Suzuki et al., PLoS Negl Trop Dis., 2017)
    • Blood meals infected with Dengue virus 1 (Raquin et al., PLoS Negl Trop Dis. , 2017)
  • The VectorBase variation BioMart instance is now scheduled to be retired after the April 2019 VectorBase release. The variation BioMart has been replaced with the new variation search facility which is described in this tutorial. If the retirement of the variation BioMart is likely to cause you any problems please contact us via the helpdesk.
  • Lots of new PopBio data has been added, and previously existing abundance data has been updated with information about attractants used in the traps. PopBio projects now have tags and data usage license information which can be used in site search and the map.
  • The PopBio map legend and data coloring behaviour has been changed to dynamically reflect the data currently visible
  • New Tour video tutorial and new PopBio map sample queries.
  • The schedule of the upcoming outreach activities, for the end of this year 2018, is posted in this page. These activities include public and private webinars and workshops. We will also be attending two scientific meetings (ASTMH/TropMed & ESA), come to our posters and talks, or stop by our exhibit booths for a personalized help desk appointments.
